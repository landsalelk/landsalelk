# Understanding Stamp Duty and Legal Fees When Buying Land in Sri Lanka (2025)

> **Key Takeaways:**
> *   **Total "Hidden" Costs:** Expect to pay **5-6%** on top of the land price.
> *   **Stamp Duty:** 3% for the first Rs. 100,000, and **4%** for the remainder.
> *   **Legal Fees:** Standard rate is **1%** of the transaction value.
> *   **Penalty:** Failing to pay Stamp Duty on time attracts massive fines.

When budgeting for a land purchase, most people focus solely on the negotiated price of the land. They often forget the "closing costs"â€”the mandatory taxes and professional fees required to legally transfer ownership.

These extra costs can amount to hundreds of thousands of rupees. In this guide, we provide a complete breakdown of **Stamp Duty**, **Legal Fees**, and **Registration Charges** effective in 2025.

## 1. Stamp Duty (The Government Tax)

Stamp Duty is a revenue tax payable to the Provincial Council where the land is located. It is the single largest "hidden" cost in any real estate transaction.

### How is it Calculated?
Stamp Duty is calculated on the **Declared Value** of the deed OR the **Government Valuation**, whichever is higher.
*   **First Rs. 100,000 of value:** Taxed at **3%**.
*   **Balance Value:** Taxed at **4%**.

### The Calculation Example ðŸ§®
Let's assume you are buying a land plot for **Rs. 10 Million (1 Crores)**.

1.  **First 100k:** 100,000 x 3% = **Rs. 3,000**
2.  **Remaining 9.9M:** 9,900,000 x 4% = **Rs. 396,000**
3.  **Total Stamp Duty:** **Rs. 399,000**

> **âš ï¸ Important:** You usually cannot pay this in cash at the Land Registry. It acts as a direct bank deposit or pay order to the Provincial Council.

## 2. Lawyer's / Notary's Fees

In Sri Lanka, a deed is only valid if attested by a Notary Public. The Notary (usually your lawyer) charges a fee for:
1.  Checking the Title (Pedigree).
2.  Drafting the new Deed.
3.  Attesting the signature and handling registration.

*   **Standard Rate:** The Bar Association of Sri Lanka guidelines suggest roughly **1%** of the transaction value.
*   **Negotiation:** For very high-value properties (e.g., 50 Million+), lawyers may agree to a lower percentage (e.g., 0.5% - 0.75%).
*   **Minimum Fee:** For very small values, lawyers may have a minimum fixed fee (e.g., Rs. 25,000).

> **Example:** For the same Rs. 10 Million land, Legal Fees would be approx **Rs. 100,000**.

## 3. Land Registry Fees

After signing, the deed must be registered to make it public record.
*   **Registration Fee:** A nominal fee (approx Rs. 500 - 1,000).
*   **Search Fees:** Expenses for obtaining extracts (Paththiru) to check the history. Usually Rs. 2,000 - 5,000 depending on how many years back you check.

## 4. Total Closing Cost Calculator

## 5. Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Who pays the Stamp Duty?**
A: Legally, the **Buyer** pays the Stamp Duty and Legal Fees. The Seller usually pays the Broker's commission (3%).

**Q: Can I undervalue the deed to save tax?**
A: This is illegal and risky. If the Land Registry determines the declared value is lower than the market value, they will impose a penalty and force you to pay the deficit. Also, "undervaluing" hurts you when you sell later, as you will face higher Capital Gains Tax exposure (if implemented).

**Q: When must I pay?**
A: Stamp Duty must be paid *before* or *on the day* of signing the deed.
